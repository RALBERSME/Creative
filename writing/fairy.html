Do you know this fairy tale?


Really? Better check it once more:


He snorted angrily through his nose and spat on the ground in front of him. He stared again at the dial of his Chinese Rolex. No question, 8 a.m. was long past and there was no trace of the cheeky brat. What happened? Had she not found the way? Lost in the forest again? Women and a sense of direction simply didn't go together...plus, today's young people could no longer be relied on. The grizzled wolf shook his head in frustration. How he had been looking forward to the fresh meat two hours ago, how he longed for it so much, when he - according to the Brothers Grimm script - choked down his porous and emaciated grandmother. The old lady certainly wasn't tasty. Neither in appearance nor in taste. But, the wolf had consoled himself as he burped, the dashing girl would compensate him for it, yes, he would have to compensate him! Otherwise, the fairytale uncles might like him in the future.

“The Wolf and the 3 Little Pigs”, “The Wolf and the 7 Little Kids”, “Little Red Riding Hood” were all pieces that took a toll on his health and gave him increasingly less joy. So he had had to stumble around all day in his grandmother's musty, old-fashioned nightgown and now dinner was canceled too? That was shit! He heard his stomach growling and felt the deep hole in his stomach. it could not go on like that. Tomorrow, he vowed, he would go on an indefinite sit-in. But tonight he still needed something to bite.

It did not help. He had to look for Little Red Riding Hood. He quickly took the forest path to Käppchensrot. Twilight had set in and the shadows were getting longer and longer. Every few meters the wolf stopped and sniffed. It smelled of fresh spruce leaves and pine greens, but not of her, the sweet thing. The pale moonlight showed him the way across the clearing. But there were no signs of the busty beauty here either. Whenever the wolf thought of her, his mouth watered. Full of greed, he now began to call her name: “Red Riding Hood, Little Cap, Little Dove, where are you?” Full of excitement, he forgot all the stage directions. How many times had the sound engineer warned him to speak in a sonorous, high voice, just like his grandmother did. But no, he bawled as he pleased. And so it happened that the hunter heard him.

With the loaded rifle, the hunter crept up to the wolf from behind. His thumb unlocked the gun. Then the hunter aimed at the wolf. But what he saw through his night vision glass robbed him of all his concentration: a full-grown, stocky wolf in a crocheted pointed cap and squeezed into a frilly nightgown that was too small, limping in front of him in pink felt slippers and pining for his beloved.

“How touching,” grinned the hunter, “whether he is dessed?” In short, he decided to try a test. So he crept into the bushes, disguised his voice and breathed gently: “here, here my darling, I am here”. Then he changed hiding places and soon “Darling, here” was heard from across the street. The wolf's eyes got bigger, the drool flowed from his mouth and he jumped straight towards the first bush. But already the voice sounded behind him. He turned around quickly. But now it echoed from the left, then from the right... the wolf opened its mouth and pounced on the creature next to him.

Bang!

A shot rang out. Then there was peace. Mouse silence. The silence of uncertainty lasted a long time. Very long. It should first be interrupted by a quiet melody. It was the singing of a young girl: “boom bumm tschacka, tschacka, hey”. Ok, the singing wasn't that quiet after all. Little Red Riding Hood sang the lyrics at the top of her lungs as she whizzed through the forest on her moped. As a poor student, she had to rely on the money that the pizza service paid her for the trip to her grandmother's. That's why she allowed herself to be persuaded to deliver the Quattro Formaggio at a late hour.

She was lucky that night, because the shot made the wolf aware of the hunter's presence and now feared for his life. He therefore preferred to remain silent in the bushes until the end of filming. Meanwhile, the hunter bled to death - two meters away from him. He had gotten tangled up in his own rifle.

And the moral of the story: Women are less likely to get lost in the forest than you think.
